About Me

I started building websites professionally in the late ninties, completing my recovery from the shock of having woken up one morning as the owner of a degree in liberal arts. Since that first opportunity with a non-profit group, I’ve worked for companies ranging from a Forture 100 company to a startup with a four-person development group. I’m currently putting this range of experience to work for clients as a freelance web/UI designer and developer.

I’m based in New York, having recently relocated from Pittsburgh to just north of the GWB in Upstate Manhattan, where I live with my wife, our almost-toddling son, and a Weimaraner who’s sure that she’s an appropriate size for an NYC apartment.

Designer “slash” Developer

Over the past fifteen (!) years, I’ve spent a lot of time straddling the line between the roles of designer and developer — juggling left brain and right brain work to build usable, functional sites and applications.

Front- & Back-End Development

Equally — if differently — creative, I’ve done a range development work: building working web pages from design mockups in HTML/CSS; providing user interaction and feedback with Javascript and libraries like jQuery and YUI; and developing full-fledged applications with PHP and MySQL.
